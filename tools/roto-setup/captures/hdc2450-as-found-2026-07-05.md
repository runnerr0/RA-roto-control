## Command priority (CPRI) — FACTORY DEFAULT

| Slot | Value | Meaning |
|------|-------|---------|
| CPRI 1 | 0 | Serial/USB (highest) |
| CPRI 2 | 1 | RC pulse |
| CPRI 3 | 2 | **Analog (lowest)** |

→ Matches the datasheet default "1-RS232, 2-RC, 3-Analog." **Analog is the lowest-priority
command.** Any serial `!G` or RC signal overrides the analog command. Relevant later: the
future serial-command path can take over *without* a config change, because serial outranks analog.

## Analog inputs (as-found)

| Input | Pin | Decode8 out | AMOD | AINA | AMIN | ACTR | AMAX | ADB | Role (interpreted) |
|-------|-----|-------------|------|------|------|------|------|-----|--------------------|
| ANA1 | 15 | out 2 | 0 | **16** | 250 | 2500 | 4750 | 5 | non-command (no channel bit) |
| ANA2 | 16 | out 3 | 0 | **48** | 250 | 2500 | 4750 | 5 | non-command (no channel bit) |
| ANA3 | 4  | out 4 | 0 | **33** | 100 | 1270 | 2500 | 0 | **Motor 1 command** — this is the DEAD pin |
| ANA4 | 17 | out 5 | 1 | **33** | 100 | 1275 | 2400 | 0 | **Motor 1 command** — the WORKING input |
| ANA5 | 21 | — | 0 | 0 | — | — | — | — | unused (unwired) |
| ANA6 | 22 | — | 0 | 0 | — | — | — | — | unused (unwired) |

**AINA decode (interpreted — verify against manual):** low bit = motor-channel mask.
`33 = 0x21` → bit0 set → **Motor Channel 1 command**. `16`/`48` have no channel bit → not
motor commands (some monitor/feedback action). So **both ANA3 (pin 4) and ANA4 (pin 17) are
assigned to command Motor 1**; ANA1/ANA2 are not motor commands.

**Calibration note:** ANA3/ANA4 use a compressed window — center ≈ **1.27 V** (stop), max ≈
**2.4 V** (full forward), min 100 mV (full reverse). The Decode8 outputs 0–5 V, so with this
calibration: DMX 0 → full reverse, DMX ≈25 % → stop, DMX ≈47 %+ → pinned full forward. Control
lives in the lower ~half of the DMX range. (ANA1/ANA2 use a wider 250/2500/4750 mV window.)

## Motor channels

| Key | M1 | M2 | Meaning |
|-----|----|----|---------|
| MMOD | 0 | 0 | open-loop (no encoder) |
| MAC | 500 | 20000 | acceleration ramp |
| MDEC | 500 | 20000 | deceleration ramp |
| MXPF | 100 | 50 | max power forward (M1 full, M2 half) |
| MXPR | 100 | 50 | max power reverse |
| MXRPM | 1000 | 1000 | max RPM reference (open-loop scaling) |
| ALIM | 1000 | 1000 | amps limit ≈ **100.0 A** per channel |
| ATGA | 16 | 0 | amps-trigger action (M1 set, M2 none) |

→ **Motor 1 is the commissioned motor** (full power, both wired analog inputs target it). Motor 2
is secondary (half power). Strongly implies the physical motor is on the **M1** output.

## What this means for the plan (no changes made yet)

1. **ANA4 (pin 17, the working input) is already a Motor 1 command.** So DMX5 → ANA4 → **Motor 1**
   should already drive the motor — your "just use analog 4" idea is essentially already wired in
   config. No remap needed to command Motor 1 from ANA4.
2. **ANA3 (pin 4, the dead/floating pin) is ALSO a Motor 1 command.** That is a hazard: a floating
   input assigned to the motor can fight ANA4 for control. The one clean change to make (later,
   deliberately) is **`AINA 3 = 0`** to remove the dead pin's authority over Motor 1.
3. **Analog is lowest command priority** — fine now (nothing sends serial/RC), and convenient for
   the future serial takeover.
4. Protection is real (100 A limit, 45 V OV, 5 V UV) — do not treat this as a blank controller.

**Proposed single change when ready:** `^AINA 3 0` (RAM only, verify, power-cycle test) so the
working ANA4 is the sole Motor-1 analog command. Everything else can stay as-found for a first
motor-in-the-loop test.
